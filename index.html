Our Focus

Across the world, the ability of governments to deliver essential services is uneven and contested. In some places, state capacity is robust. In others, it's fragile or entirely absent. Everywhere, it's under pressure.

Yet conversations about state capacity often remain abstract - disconnected from the operational realities that determine whether institutions can execute what they promise. At a moment when confidence in government is fragile and anti-government rhetoric dominates public discourse, we need better ways to see how competent delivery actually happens.y.
